# Fish Behavior Patterns Database

This document contains comprehensive fish behavior patterns used by the FishSmart Pro AI engine to generate accurate fishing strategies.

## Largemouth Bass (*Micropterus salmoides*)

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Spring (Pre-Spawn)**: Move shallow (3-8 ft), aggressive feeding, target spawning flats
- **Spring (Spawn)**: Bedding behavior, protect nests, use slow presentations
- **Spring (Post-Spawn)**: Recovering phase, deeper water near spawning areas
- **Summer**: Deep structure (15-25 ft), early morning/late evening activity
- **Fall**: Shallow feeding frenzy, follow baitfish, aggressive patterns
- **Winter**: Deep, slow metabolism, finesse techniques required

### Weather Influences
- **Barometric Pressure**: High pressure = deeper, tighter to cover; Low pressure = active, roaming
- **Fronts**: Pre-frontal = excellent feeding; Post-frontal = tough bite, go deep
- **Wind**: Creates current, positions fish on wind-blown points
- **Temperature**: Ideal range 65-75°F, below 50°F = slow down

### Preferred Structure
- Vegetation (lily pads, hydrilla, milfoil)
- Wood cover (laydowns, stumps, brush piles)
- Rock piles and ledges
- Docks and boat houses
- Points and humps

## Smallmouth Bass (*Micropterus dolomieu*)

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Spring**: Rocky areas, 5-15 ft, crayfish patterns
- **Summer**: Deep rock structure (20-40 ft), current breaks
- **Fall**: Shallow rocks, aggressive feeding
- **Winter**: Deep, slow presentations, drop-shot techniques

### Weather Influences
- **Clear Water**: Spooky, use lighter line, natural colors
- **Cloudy Days**: More active, roam further from cover
- **Wind**: Position on wind-blown rocky points
- **Temperature**: Prefer cooler water (60-70°F)

### Preferred Structure
- Rocky shorelines and points
- Gravel bars
- Deep ledges and drop-offs
- Current breaks
- Weed edges in clear water

## Walleye (*Sander vitreus*)

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Spring**: Shallow spawning areas (5-15 ft), night fishing effective
- **Summer**: Deep structure (20-40 ft), thermocline, suspended fish
- **Fall**: Shallow feeding, follow baitfish schools
- **Winter**: Deep holes, very slow presentations

### Weather Influences
- **Low Light**: Dawn, dusk, overcast days, night fishing
- **Wind**: Creates mud lines, positions fish
- **Temperature**: Ideal 55-65°F, avoid extremes
- **Clear Water**: Go deeper, use natural presentations

### Preferred Structure
- Rocky points and reefs
- Drop-offs and breaks
- Weed edges
- Mud lines
- Deep holes

## Northern Pike (*Esox lucius*)

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Spring**: Shallow spawning (3-10 ft), very aggressive
- **Summer**: Weed beds (10-20 ft), ambush points
- **Fall**: Shallow weeds, following baitfish
- **Winter**: Deep, slow, large baits

### Weather Influences
- **Cold Water**: More active, prefer 50-65°F
- **Overcast**: Excellent conditions, roam freely
- **Wind**: Positions on wind-blown weed edges
- **Pressure Changes**: Active before fronts

### Preferred Structure
- Weed beds and edges
- Rocky points
- Drop-offs near structure
- Shallow bays (spring)
- Deep weed lines (summer)

## General Fishing Principles

### Solunar Influence
- **Major Feeding Periods**: Moonrise/moonset, sunrise/sunset
- **Minor Feeding Periods**: Mid-morning, mid-afternoon
- **Moon Phase**: New/full moon = stronger activity periods

### Barometric Pressure
- **Rising Pressure**: Fish become active, feed aggressively
- **High Pressure**: Fish tight to cover, deeper
- **Falling Pressure**: Excellent feeding before storm
- **Low Pressure**: Tough bite, fish lethargic

### Water Clarity
- **Clear**: Lighter line, natural colors, finesse presentations
- **Stained**: Darker colors, vibration, scent
- **Muddy**: Large baits, noise, strong scent

### Wind
- **No Wind**: Fish scattered, surface fishing good
- **Light Wind**: Positions fish on points, current edges
- **Moderate Wind**: Creates mud lines, active feeding
- **Heavy Wind**: Fish deep, difficult boat control

### Temperature
- **Cold Water (<50°F)**: Slow down, deep, small baits
- **Cool Water (50-65°F)**: Active, moderate speeds
- **Ideal Water (65-75°F)**: Peak activity, variety of techniques
- **Warm Water (>75°F)**: Early/late, deep, oxygenated areas

### Time of Day
- **Dawn**: Prime feeding time, shallow activity
- **Mid-Morning**: Good bite, moving deeper
- **Mid-Day**: Tough bite, go deep or shade
- **Late Afternoon**: Good bite, activity increases
- **Dusk**: Prime feeding time, shallow activity
- **Night**: Excellent for certain species (catfish, walleye)
